About Air Quality Index

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 0 to 50 is said to be good.

Air quality is satisfactory, and air pollution poses little or no risk.

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 51 to 100 is said to be moderate.

Air quality is acceptable. However, there may be a risk for some people, particularly those who are unusually sensitive to air pollution.

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 101 to 150 is said to be unhealthy for sensitive group of people.

Members of sensitive groups may experience health effects. The general public is less likely to be affected.

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 151 to 200 is said to be unhealthy.

Some members of the general public may experience health effects, members of sensitive groups may experience more serious health effects.

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 201 to 300 is said to be very unhealthy.

Health alert: The risk of health effects is increased for everyone.

If the air quality index (AQI) ranges from 301 and higher is said to be hazardous.

Health warning of emergency conditions: everyone is more likely to be affected.
